The Mandate

You are operating as a Junior Analyst at a wealth management firm. Your team has been assigned three client profiles and $500,000 in hypothetical assets under management. Over the course of the semester, you will build, stress test, and defend a fully optimized investment portfolio for each client.

This is not a textbook exercise. Your clients have specific goals, constraints, and risk limits. Your portfolio must satisfy all three.

Team Structure

Each team has four members. Divide the analytical work using the roles below. Every member contributes to the final pitch — there are no passengers.

⚠ No Silos

All four roles feed into one unified portfolio. If your macro forecast says inflation is falling, your portfolio cannot hold inflation hedges.

Consistency between phases is graded. Contradictions between the macro thesis and portfolio construction will cost points in both.

AI Rules of Engagement

AI tools are permitted — and expected — but with clear limits. Every team must submit an AI Audit Log documenting all prompts used and how AI output was verified or revised.

⚠ The Hallucination Clause

Submitting unverified AI-generated data (prices, ratios, forecasts) is treated as professional negligence and results in point deductions regardless of whether the numbers look plausible.

Every AI-generated number must be traceable to a human-verified source: FactSet, FRED, or your own Excel model.

Phase 1
Macro Foundations — Top-Down Forecast
Objective: Build a shared macroeconomic view that drives every portfolio decision your team makes for the rest of the semester. All three clients use the same macro thesis.
What to do
  • Analyze the current economic environment using Real GDP, CPI inflation, the 10Y-2Y yield curve spread, consumer sentiment, and the Federal Funds Rate.
  • Identify where we are in the economic cycle: Expansion, Late-Cycle, Recession, or Recovery.
  • Define three scenarios — Bull Case, Base Case, and Bear Case — and assign a probability to each. Probabilities must sum to 100%.
  • State a sector outlook: which sectors are favored and which are avoided given your Base Case.
  • Write a concluding investment thesis (one paragraph) that will anchor Phase 3.
Scenario probability guidance
  • Base Case: 50–60% — your most likely outcome
  • Bull Case: 15–25% — optimistic growth, easing inflation
  • Bear Case: 20–25% — recessionary pressure or stagflation
Data sources
  • FRED (fred.stlouisfed.org): GDP, CPI, Fed Funds Rate, 10Y-2Y Spread, Consumer Sentiment
  • FactSet: Type @ECO for GDP and CPI consensus, @YIELD for the live yield curve, @ECON for historical trends
  • CME FedWatch Tool: Market-implied probability of rate changes

Phase 2
Client Guardrails — Investment Policy Statement (IPS)
Objective: Define the investment rules for each of your three clients before touching a single security. The IPS is the legal and ethical foundation for every decision that follows.
What to do

Complete a full IPS for each of the three assigned clients. The IPS must address all seven RRTTLLU constraints:

⚠ The Hard Constraint — Maximum Acceptable Drawdown

You must state an explicit maximum drawdown limit for each client (e.g., −10%). This number is your Tripwire.

If your portfolio exceeds this limit in the Phase 5 stress test, you have breached the mandate. You must re-allocate or add a hedge to bring the portfolio back within bounds.

A portfolio that fails the stress test and does not correct itself earns zero points in Phase 5.

Phase 3
The Bridge — CME Matrix & Portfolio Optimization
Objective: Translate the Phase 1 macro thesis into mathematical asset allocation targets. This is where the story meets the spreadsheet.
What to do
  • Build the Capital Market Expectations (CME) Matrix in Excel — a table of expected returns and standard deviations for each asset class (equities, fixed income, cash) under your Base Case.
  • Build a second CME Matrix for your Bear Case. Note that correlations between asset classes typically converge toward 1.0 during a market panic.
  • Use Excel Solver to find the Maximum Sharpe Ratio portfolio for each client. Each client will have a different optimal allocation based on their IPS constraints.
  • The resulting allocation is your starting portfolio for Phase 4.
Excel functions required
  • MMULT and TRANSPOSE — for matrix multiplication in the covariance matrix
  • STDEV.S, CORREL, COVARIANCE.S — for computing portfolio risk
  • Excel Solver — maximize the Sharpe Ratio by adjusting weights; add constraints for each client's IPS limits
→ The Connection Rule

Your Phase 3 expected returns must be consistent with your Phase 1 thesis.

Example: If Phase 1 predicts high inflation, your fixed income expected return in the CME Matrix must reflect higher yields — not a low-rate assumption. Graders will check for consistency between the macro narrative and the matrix numbers.

Phase 4
Security Selection & Risk Mitigation
Objective: Fill the optimized portfolio with real securities. Every pick must be justified by your macro thesis. Then add a mandatory hedge.
What to do
  • Select 10 securities or ETFs per client — individual equities, bond ETFs, sector ETFs, REITs, or commodities.
  • Apply the Macro Filter: every security must pass through your Phase 1 thesis. If you are bearish on consumer discretionary, you cannot hold a consumer discretionary stock.
  • Write a 1-page Investment Thesis for each security explaining why it is appropriate for this specific client given the macro environment.
  • Implement a mandatory derivative hedge. The most common options are a Protective Put (floor on potential losses) or a Covered Call (caps upside to reduce cost basis). Document the cost of the hedge and how it modifies the risk profile.
Selection requirements
  • Buy-and-hold focus — no day trading or market timing
  • Minimum diversification: at least 3 distinct asset classes represented
  • Each security thesis must cite a data source (FactSet, FRED, or SEC filing)
  • Derivative choice must be explained relative to alternatives — why this hedge and not another?

Phase 5
The Stress Test & Quantitative Audit
Objective: Run the optimized portfolio through your Bear Case scenario and verify it stays within each client's IPS drawdown limit. If it fails, fix it.
What to do
  • Apply your Phase 1 Bear Case return assumptions to the Phase 4 portfolio to simulate a market crash.
  • Calculate the projected portfolio loss in dollar and percentage terms for each client.
  • Compare the simulated loss to the IPS drawdown limit (the Tripwire). If the loss exceeds the limit, re-allocate to cash or add a hedge to bring the portfolio within bounds.
  • Report the final risk metrics for each client's portfolio.
Required metrics to report
  • Portfolio Beta — sensitivity to market movements
  • Portfolio Standard Deviation — total volatility
  • Sharpe Ratio — return per unit of total risk
⚠ Stress Test Pass / Fail Criteria

PASS: Simulated Bear Case loss is within the IPS drawdown limit, or the team has documented a specific re-allocation that brings it within bounds.

FAIL: Simulated loss exceeds the drawdown limit and no corrective action is taken. This is a mandate breach.

Graders will inspect the Excel model to verify the stress test uses dynamic formulas — not hard-coded numbers.

Phase 6
Final Pitch — Dossier & Oral Defense
Objective: Present your work to the Investment Committee. You have two audiences — a compliance reviewer who will check every number, and executives who need to trust you in 30 seconds.
The Two-Audience Approach
Written Submission

Audience: Risk Managers & Compliance

Focus: Technical precision, correct math, CFA-level metrics

Format: 20–30 slide Technical Dossier

Oral Presentation

Audience: Clients & Investment Committee

Focus: Persuasion, narrative, trust

Format: 15–20 minute pitch + 5–10 min Q&A

Technical Dossier — 9 required sections
  • Executive Summary: One-paragraph BLUF — Bull, Base, or Bear conviction, target GDP/CPI, primary driver
  • Macro Thesis: Phase 1 scenario table with probabilities
  • Client Profile Summaries: IPS highlights for all three clients on one page
  • Asset Allocation: CME Matrix and Solver weights for each client
  • Security Selection: Ticker, weight, asset class, and one-line macro rationale per holding
  • Risk Metrics: Beta, standard deviation, and Sharpe Ratio in a clean comparison table
  • Stress Test Results: Pass/Fail verdict and re-allocation actions taken
  • Investment Recommendations: Final equity/bond weighting with sector bias
  • AI Audit Log: Full prompt history with human verification notes
⚠ The Q&A Is the Final Boss

30% of your oral grade comes from the 5–10 minutes of questions after you finish presenting.

The professor and your peers will play the role of the Investment Committee CIO. You will be asked to defend specific numbers, explain trade-offs, and respond to Bear Case challenges in real time.

If one student answers all questions while teammates stay silent, the team loses points for cohesion. Everyone must be able to speak to every phase.
